## 1. Who Can Enroll

Horizon Institute of Applied Studies accepts enrollments from:

- Domestic students who have completed Year 12 (or an equivalent qualification) for Diploma and Bachelor courses.
- Graduates holding a recognised undergraduate degree for Postgraduate courses.
- Mature-age applicants (21+) without Year 12 may apply for Diploma courses via the Alternative Entry Pathway, which includes a literacy and numeracy assessment.

Each course has specific prerequisites listed in the course catalogue. Meeting the minimum prerequisites does not guarantee admission for capped courses (Bachelor of Nursing and Bachelor of Computer Science (Artificial Intelligence) are capped intakes).

## 2. How to Enroll — Step by Step

1. **Choose your course and intake.** Intakes open in July, October and February each year. Check the course page for which intakes apply to your course.
2. **Submit an online application** at the Student Portal with: certified copy of your academic transcripts, photo identification, and (for Health stream courses) your immunisation record.
3. **Receive your assessment outcome** within 10 business days. Successful applicants receive a Letter of Offer.
4. **Accept your offer** by signing the Student Agreement and paying the enrollment deposit of AUD 500. The deposit is credited toward your first term fees.
5. **Complete pre-enrollment requirements.** Health stream students must provide a valid National Police Check and, where applicable, a Working With Children Check before their first clinical placement.
6. **Attend Orientation Week**, held one week before each intake start date. Attendance at orientation is compulsory for first-term students.

## 3. Enrollment Deadlines

- Applications close **15 business days** before the intake start date.
- Late applications (up to 5 business days before start) may be accepted for courses with available places, subject to a late application fee of AUD 100.
- Enrollment deposits must be paid within **10 business days** of the Letter of Offer, or the offer lapses.

## 4. Deferring an Enrollment

Students may defer their enrollment to the next available intake (maximum two consecutive deferrals) at no charge, provided the request is made before Orientation Week. Deferral requests after the course start date are treated under the Cancellation and Withdrawal Policy (HIAS-POL-002).

## 5. Changing Courses

Students may transfer to another HIAS course within the first four weeks of teaching ("the transfer window") without academic penalty. Fee differences are adjusted on a pro-rata basis. Transfers between streams (for example, Business to Health) may require additional prerequisite checks.
