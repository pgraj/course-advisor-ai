# Horizon Institute of Applied Studies (HIAS)
# Cancellation and Withdrawal Policy

*Document HIAS-POL-002 | Version 1.8 | Effective 1 June 2026*
*(Fictional institute — sample document for a portfolio project)*

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains what happens when a student cancels their enrollment, withdraws from a course, or has their enrollment cancelled by the Institute. Refund amounts are governed by the Refund and Payment Policy (HIAS-POL-003).

## 2. Student-Initiated Cancellation Before Course Start

- Cancellation **more than 20 business days before** the intake start date: full release from the Student Agreement. The AUD 500 enrollment deposit is refundable less an administration fee of AUD 100.
- Cancellation **20 business days or fewer before** the intake start date: the enrollment deposit of AUD 500 is forfeited. No other fees apply if no term fees have been paid.

All cancellations must be submitted in writing via the Student Portal. Verbal cancellations are not accepted.

## 3. Withdrawal After Course Start

- **Within the first 4 teaching weeks ("census period"):** students may withdraw without academic penalty. Term fees are refundable per the Refund and Payment Policy, less the deposit and administration fee.
- **After census and before week 8:** withdrawal is recorded as "Withdrawn — No Fail" on the transcript. Term fees for the current term are not refundable, but future terms are not charged.
- **After week 8 of a term:** withdrawal is recorded as "Withdrawn — Fail" for in-progress units unless Special Circumstances apply (see Section 5). Current term fees are not refundable.

## 4. Institute-Initiated Cancellation

HIAS may cancel a student's enrollment for:

- Non-payment of fees more than 20 business days after the due date (after two written reminders);
- Academic misconduct as determined under the Academic Integrity Policy;
- Failure to meet course progress requirements after the formal intervention process (see Academic Progress and Extension Policy, HIAS-POL-004);
- Failure to provide mandatory checks (police check, WWCC, immunisation) before clinical placement in Health stream courses.

Students receive 10 business days' written notice and may appeal to the Student Appeals Committee within that period.

## 5. Special Circumstances Withdrawal

Students who withdraw due to serious illness, bereavement of an immediate family member, or other circumstances beyond their control may apply for a Special Circumstances Withdrawal at any point in the term. If approved:

- The withdrawal is recorded as "Withdrawn — No Fail" regardless of timing;
- Term fees for the affected term are credited in full toward a future intake (valid for 24 months), or refunded at 50% if the student does not intend to return.

Supporting evidence (medical certificate or equivalent documentation) is required within 10 business days of the application.

## 6. Cancelling Individual Units

Students in Bachelor and Master courses may drop individual units before census with no fee or academic penalty. Units dropped after census follow the same rules as Section 3 on a per-unit fee basis.

## 7. Course Cancellation by HIAS

If HIAS cancels a course or intake before it starts (for example, due to insufficient enrollments), students receive a full refund of all monies paid, including the deposit, within 15 business days — or may transfer to another course or intake with a fee credit of AUD 250 as a goodwill gesture.
