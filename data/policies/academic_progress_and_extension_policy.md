# Academic Progress, Re-assessment and Extension Policy

*Document HIAS-POL-004 | Version 1.5 | Effective 1 June 2026*

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains what happens if a student cannot complete their course on time, fails a required exam or assessment, or needs an extension — and the support available at each stage.

## 2. Failing an Assessment or Exam

- **First attempt failed:** the student is automatically entitled to **one re-sit** of the failed exam or one re-submission of the failed assessment. A re-sit fee of AUD 120 per exam applies (waived for students with an approved Special Consideration). The re-sit must be taken within 20 business days of results release. The maximum mark recorded for a passed re-sit is a Pass (50%).
- **Re-sit also failed:** the student must re-enrol in the unit in the next available term and pay the per-unit fee. Failing the same unit **three times** triggers a formal Academic Progress Review.

## 3. Assignment Extensions

- Students may request an extension of **up to 5 business days** on any assignment by applying through the Student Portal **before** the due date, with a brief reason. First two extensions per term are granted automatically.
- Extensions of **6 to 20 business days** require supporting evidence (medical certificate, carer documentation, employer letter) and approval by the Unit Coordinator.
- Work submitted late without an approved extension incurs a penalty of 5% of the available marks per business day, to a maximum of 10 business days, after which the submission receives zero.

## 4. Unable to Complete the Course On Time

Students who cannot finish within the standard duration have these options:

1. **Reduced study load:** drop to part-time (minimum one unit per term). The course end date extends accordingly. Fees remain per-unit, so total cost does not increase.
2. **Leave of Absence:** up to **12 months** of approved leave per course, in blocks of one term/semester. No fees are charged during leave. Course content changes during leave may require a transition plan on return.
3. **Maximum completion timeframe:** all courses must be completed within **twice the standard duration** (for example, a 12-month Diploma must be completed within 24 months; a 3-year Bachelor within 6 years). Students approaching this limit are contacted by the Academic Progress team to agree a completion plan.

## 5. Course Extensions Beyond the Maximum Timeframe

In exceptional cases (documented serious illness, carer responsibilities, defence service), the Academic Board may approve a single extension of up to **two additional terms** beyond the maximum timeframe. Applications require:

- A written request with supporting evidence;
- A unit-by-unit completion plan endorsed by the Course Coordinator;
- Payment of any outstanding fees (an extension cannot be granted while fees are overdue).

If an extension is not approved and the timeframe lapses, the enrollment is cancelled under HIAS-POL-002 and the student receives a Statement of Attainment for completed units.

## 6. Academic Progress Review (Intervention Process)

A student enters the review process when they fail 50% or more of enrolled units in a term, or fail the same unit three times. The process has three stages:

1. **Stage 1 — Early support:** meeting with a Student Success Adviser; free tutoring and study-skills workshops offered.
2. **Stage 2 — Conditional enrollment:** a formal study plan with reduced load and mandatory adviser check-ins.
3. **Stage 3 — Show cause:** the student must demonstrate why their enrollment should continue. Possible outcomes: continued conditional enrollment, transfer to a lower-level course (for example, Bachelor to Diploma with credit for passed units), or enrollment cancellation with appeal rights.

## 7. Special Consideration

Students affected by illness or serious circumstances during an assessment period may apply for Special Consideration within 5 business days of the assessment date. Approved outcomes include: a no-fee re-sit, an extended deadline, or a "Withheld" grade pending completion.

## 8. Appeals

Any decision under this policy may be appealed in writing to the Student Appeals Committee within 20 business days. Students may be accompanied by a support person at any review meeting.
